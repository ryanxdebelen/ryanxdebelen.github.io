Ryan Anthony J. de Belen

I am a PhD student at the University of New South Wales, Computer Science and Engineering, where I develop computational models for human visual attention and gaze communication behaviour analysis, ultimately applying them for autism diagnosis.

I am currently supervised by Arcot Sowmya, Gelareh Mohammadi and Tomasz Bednarz.

Email  /  CV  /  Bio  /  Google Scholar  /  Twitter  /  Github

Research

I'm interested in computer vision, machine learning and human-centred design. Much of my research is about quantifying human visual attention and gaze communication behaviours. Representative papers are highlighted.

ScanpathNet: A Recurrent Mixture Density Network for Scanpath Prediction
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Tomasz Bednarz, Arcot Sowmya
CVPRW, 2022 (Best Paper Honourable Mention)
Paper / Video Presentation

We model human visual attention (i.e., scanpaths) using a deep learning inspired by the latest theoretical model of attention in neuroscience.

Joint Augmented Reality Video Analytics and Artificial Intelligence Supervision
Benjamin Sho, Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Rowan T Hughes, Tomasz Bednarz
SIGGRAPH Asia Posters, 2021
Paper / Video in the Supplementary Material Section /

We present an augmented reality application that explores human-AI interaction in a computer vision context by providing an integrated platform for video analytics and AI model fine-tuning.
EyeXplain autism: interactive system for eye tracking data analysis and deep neural network interpretation for autism spectrum disorder diagnosis
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Tomasz Bednarz, Arcot Sowmya
CHI, 2021  
Paper / Video Presentation / Poster

We present EyeXplain Autism, an interactive system that enables physicians to analyse eye tracking data, perform automated diagnosis and interpret deep neural network predictions.
Computer vision in autism spectrum disorder research: a systematic review of published studies from 2009 to 2019
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen Tomasz Bednarz, Arcot Sowmya, Dennis Del Favero
Translational Psychiatry, 2020
Paper

We examine how computer vision analysis has been useful in autism spectrum disorder diagnosis, therapy and autism research in general.
Integrating mixed reality and internet of things as an assistive technology for elderly people living in a smart home
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Tomasz Bednarz, Dennis Del Favero
VRCAI, 2019  
Paper

We present a wearable assitive technology that follows interaction design guidelines to ensure reliability, usability, and suitability for everyday use.
Combining mixed reality and Internet of Things: an interaction design research on developing assistive technologies for elderly people
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Tomasz Bednarz, Dennis Del Favero
HCII, 2019   Silver Award - Student Design Competition
Paper / Video

We propose a wearable assistive technology, which is a combination of Mixed Reality and Internet of Things technologies, that aims to improve the Quality of Life (QoL) and to maintain the self-independence of people aged 65 or above.

Mixed Reality and Internet of Things (MRIoT) Interface Design Guidelines for Elderly People
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Tomasz Bednarz
IV, 2019
Paper

To address the usability needs and reconcile inconsistencies among the design strategies and technology characteristics, we consulted relevant design criteria and extended these associated concepts to include Mixed Reality and Internet of Things interfaces for the ageing population.

A systematic review of the current state of collaborative mixed reality technologies: 2013–2018
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Huyen Nguyen, Daniel Filonik, Dennis Del Favero, Tomasz Bednarz
AIMS Electronics and Electrical Engineering, 2018
Paper

Although a considerable amount of research has already been done to support collaboration between users in MR, there is still no systematic review to determine the current state of collaborative MR applications. In this paper, collaborative MR studies published from 2013 to 2018 were reviewed.

ANEEME: interactive mesh cutting using 3D snakes for automatic skeletal rigging of scanned character models
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Maria Isabel S Saludares, Rowel O Atienza,
i3D, 2017
Paper / Video in the Supplementary Material Section

ANEEME is a system for automatic skeletal rigging of 3D scanned humanoid objects such as toys, figurines, sculptures and even humans. Unlike other automatic skeletal rigging techniques, ANEEME does not require humanoid objects to be in a specific pose, (e.g. T-Pose) before scanning and rigging.

Automatic skeleton generation using hierarchical mesh segmentation
Ryan Anthony J. de Belen, Rowel O Atienza
SIGGRAPH Asia, 2016
Paper / Video

With the goal of bringing any humanoid toys to life, a pipeline is presented to automatically generate the toy's skeletal structure and skinning weights.
